
    Q: What is the objective of the B.Des. in Interaction Design at JKLU?
    A: The programme caters to a digitally adept generation, offering a blend of cognitive sciences and computer sciences, to design user-centric digital experiences.

    Q: What are the career prospects for B.Des. in Interaction Design graduates?
    A: Graduates can work as Interaction designers in technology companies, app developers, UX designers for software companies, or pursue roles in service design.

    Q: What is taught in the B.Des. Interaction Design programme?
    A: Courses include cognitive ergonomics, human-computer interaction, UI/UX design, quick prototyping, typography, layouts, and more.
