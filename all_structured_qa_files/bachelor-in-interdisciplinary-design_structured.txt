
    Q: What is the objective of the B.Des. in Interdisciplinary Design at JKLU?
    A: The program combines new-age domains and traditional approaches, providing students with knowledge from various design disciplines as well as entrepreneurship and technology, preparing them for cross-functional roles.

    Q: What are the career prospects for B.Des. in Interdisciplinary Design graduates?
    A: Graduates find opportunities in design firms, creative agencies, and startups, and they are well-positioned to start their own agencies or specialize further in their chosen fields.

    Q: What is taught in the B.Des. Interdisciplinary Design programme?
    A: Courses include Visual Design, Product Design, Interaction Design, and Spatial Design, with a focus on problem-solving, form and aesthetics, and user-centric design.
